Hamburger Recipe

Description

Nothing beats a classic, juicy homemade hamburger. This recipe delivers a perfectly seared, savory beef patty nestled in a toasted bun, complemented by crisp vegetables and a signature tangy burger sauce. It is the ultimate crowd-pleaser for backyard barbecues or a quick weeknight dinner, offering a timeless balance of textures and rich, smoky flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) 80/20 Ground Beef
  • Salt to taste
  • Black Pepper: ½ teaspoon, freshly cracked
  • Garlic Powder: ½ teaspoon (optional)
  • Hamburger Buns: 4 brioche or sesame seed buns
  • Butter: 1 tablespoon
  • Cheese: 4 slices of Cheddar or American cheese (optional)
  • Toppings: Crisp lettuce, sliced tomatoes, red onion rings, and pickles
  • Condiments: Ketchup, mustard, or mayonnaise

Steps

  1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions. Gently shape each portion into a ball, then press it down into a patty about ¾-inch thick and slightly wider than your buns. Press a small indentation into the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent it from puffing up while cooking.
  2. Generously season both sides of the patties with salt, black pepper, and garlic powder just before cooking.
  3. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Spread a thin layer of butter on the cut sides of the buns and place them face-down on the skillet. Toast for 1 to 2 minutes until golden brown, then set aside.
  4. Turn the heat up to medium-high. Place the patties in the hot skillet (or on a preheated grill). Cook undisturbed for 3 to 4 minutes until a deep, flavorful brown crust forms on the bottom.
  5. Flip the patties over. If you are making cheeseburgers, place a slice of cheese on top of each patty immediately. Cook for another 3 minutes until the cheese is melted and the burger reaches your desired level of doneness.
  6. Spread your preferred condiments on the toasted bottom bun. Layer on the lettuce, tomato, and onion. Place the juicy patty on top, finish with pickles and the top bun, and serve immediately.
