Biryani Recipe

Description

Biryani is a layered rice dish made by combining long-grain basmati rice with spiced meat or vegetables, aromatics, and herbs. It is typically prepared by partially cooking rice and a separate curry base, then layering them and finishing with a dum (slow-steam) process so the flavors penetrate deeply while keeping the rice grains distinct and fragrant.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 400-500 g chicken (or mutton), cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped (or ½ cup yogurt instead/in addition)
  • 3-4 tbsp cooking oil or ghee
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, 3-4 cardamom pods
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse basmati rice until water runs clear. Soak for 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pot. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half for garnish.
  3. Add whole spices and sauté for 30-60 seconds until aromatic.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cook briefly until raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chicken pieces, turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until chicken starts browning.
  6. Add tomatoes and cook until soft, or mix in yogurt for a richer base. Simmer until chicken is partially cooked and gravy thickens.
  7. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil with salt and whole spices if desired. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain immediately.
  8. Layer the biryani: place half the rice over the cooked chicken, add fried onions, coriander, mint, and a bit of garam masala. Repeat layers.
  9. Drizzle saffron milk (if using) and a little ghee on top.
  10. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes.
  11. Rest for 10 minutes, then gently fluff and serve.
