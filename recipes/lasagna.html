Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a layered oven-baked pasta dish traditionally made with sheets of pasta, meat or vegetable sauce, cheese, and sometimes béchamel. It is assembled in alternating layers, then baked until the top is golden and bubbling, allowing the flavors to meld into a rich, structured dish with soft interior layers and a slightly crisp crust.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna sheets (fresh or dried)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 500 g ground beef (or mixed beef/pork)
  • 800 g canned tomatoes (crushed or passata)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil (or Italian seasoning)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 250 g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 50-80 g parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 500 ml milk (for béchamel, optional but common)
  • Nutmeg (optional, for béchamel)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until soft. Add garlic and sauté briefly.
  2. Add ground meat and cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20-30 minutes until thickened.
  4. For béchamel (optional): melt butter in a saucepan, whisk in flour, cook for 1-2 minutes, then slowly add milk while whisking until smooth and thick. Add salt and a pinch of nutmeg.
  5. Cook lasagna sheets if required by package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  6. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  7. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce at the bottom. Add pasta sheets, followed by meat sauce, béchamel (if using), and a mix of mozzarella and parmesan.
  8. Repeat layering until ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and a final cheese layer on top.
  9. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 15-20 minutes until top is golden and bubbling.
  10. Rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing to allow layers to set.
